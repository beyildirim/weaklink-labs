The executive briefing should be exactly one page when printed. Executives
will not read more than that. Focus on:

1. **The problem** (2-3 sentences): Why supply chain security matters.
   Reference real incidents (SolarWinds, Log4Shell, xz-utils).

2. **What you are doing** (table): The six pillars, one line each.
   Governance, Tooling, Training, Monitoring, Incident Response,
   Continuous Improvement.

3. **Timeline** (table): Four rows. 30 days, 90 days, 6 months, 1 year.
   Each row: 1-2 key deliverables. Not details, milestones.

4. **Investment** (table): What it costs. Tooling, headcount, training,
   external audits. Year 1 total.

5. **Risk if we do nothing** (table): What happens if this gets deprioritized.
   Federal contract risk, breach probability, customer trust.

6. **The ask**: One sentence. "We need X budget approved and Y person
   assigned by Z date."

Avoid jargon. "SLSA Level 3" means nothing to a CFO. Translate to
business outcomes: "Tamper-proof build records that prove our software
was not modified after development."
